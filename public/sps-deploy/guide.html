SPS DEPLOY — Quick Start

Track the crews reporting to you — check-ins, daily 214s & DTRs, contacts, and notes for end-of-tour reviews. All on your phone, works with zero bars.

▶ Open SPS Deploy

iPhone: open in Safari → Share → Add to Home Screen → it works like an app, even offline.

Start of deployment — 2 minutes

  1. Fire number — the app asks when you first open it. Start typing and it finds the fire on the BCWS map and fills the name for you. Complex or MZOC? Pick the type and add each fire you work under it.
  2. Add your crews — two ways:
    • 📷 Scan T-Card — photo of the T-cards on your board; crew names, callsigns and leaders fill in automatically. You review before anything saves. No setup needed. (Needs cell service; offline, the photo still attaches and you type the details.)
    • ＋ Add crew — 30 seconds by hand. Everything is editable later.
  3. Check-in schedule — default is every 2 hrs starting 08:00. Change both in ⚙︎ Settings.

Daily rhythm

📻 Check-ins
  • The home screen counts down to the next check-in — all crews check in at the same times. Goes 🔴 when overdue.
  • Tap Start check-in round, then tap each crew as they call in — time is recorded.
  • Crew doesn't answer? Mark "no contact." The app reminds you to try again every 5 min (adjustable), logs every attempt with a time, and has an Alert Fire Centre button when you decide it's time. The full timeline is saved — your record of exactly what you did and when.
📋 Paperwork (214 / DTR)
  • When a crew hands in their ICS 214 or DTR, tap the button — receipt time is recorded.
  • Crews handing in yesterday's paperwork this morning? Use the ‹ › date arrows.
  • Names colour-code themselves: green up to date · yellow 1 outstanding · red 2+ outstanding.
👥 Crews & contacts
  • Tap any crew: members with one-tap 📞 call buttons, radio channel, and a notes field — jot tasking and observations as you go; they end up in the end-of-deployment report for reviews.
  • Crew members swap mid-tour? Add/remove them anytime. Whole crew moves to another TFL? Release crew keeps their records without deleting.
  • Overhead on this fire — quick list of your Branch / Div / STAM / other TFLs with positions and call buttons.
📡 Share with overhead (optional)
  • ⚙︎ Settings → Create shared board link → text it to your STAM. They see your crews' check-in and paperwork status live, read-only, no app needed. Stop sharing anytime.

End of deployment

  1. ⚙︎ Settings → 🏁 End deployment.
  2. It backs everything up to the cloud automatically, then tap Save deployment report — one file with your full check-in log, no-contact timelines, paperwork times, and crew notes. Drop it on the incident drive or email it in.
  3. Tap Clear this device — ready for the next deployment.

Good to know

  • Works offline. Everything except T-Card auto-read and fire-number lookup works with no service. Nothing to log into.
  • Your data stays on your phone. Nothing is shared unless you create a board link. Phone numbers never leave the device.
  • Reminder sounds fire while the app is open on screen — in a real no-contact you'll have it open anyway.
  • Add to Home Screen first — it keeps the app cached for offline use.

⚠️ Personal field tool — not an official BCWS system. Radio and standard procedures always come first; this just keeps the record straight.
